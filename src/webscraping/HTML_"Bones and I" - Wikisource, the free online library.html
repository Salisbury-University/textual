"Bones and I;" or, the Sketeton at Home  (1871) 
by G. J. Whyte-Melville
  • information about this edition. 
  • sister projects: Wikidata item.

Third edition: Chapman and Hall, Picadilly. First published in 1868.

An anomaly to the corpus of Whyte-Melville's work, since it is far from the worlds of the hunting field or the historical romance. Instead Bones and I centres upon an urban recluse who lives in a small, modern villa situated in a London cul de sac looking out upon 'the dead wall at the back of an hospital.'
