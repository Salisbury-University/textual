<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg/220px-Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="170" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg/330px-Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg/440px-Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="925" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Erland-von-Koch-1952.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Erland von Koch at his summer residence in <a href="/wiki/Orn%C3%B6" title="Ornö">Ornö</a>, Sweden, in 1952.</div></div></div>
<p><b>Sigurd Christian Jag Erland Vogt von Koch</b> (26 April 1910 – 31 January 2009) was a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He wrote symphonies, ballets, an opera, and other compositions, including music for film.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erland_von_Koch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> as the son of composer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigurd_von_Koch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sigurd von Koch (page does not exist)">Sigurd von Koch</a> (1879–1919), Erland von Koch studied at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Music,_Stockholm" title="Royal College of Music, Stockholm">Stockholm Conservatory</a> from 1931 to 1935 and subsequently passed the advanced choirmaster and <a href="/wiki/Organ_(music)" title="Organ (music)">organist</a> examinations. Between 1936 and 1938, he lived in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in order to pursue studies in <a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">composition</a> with <a href="/wiki/Paul_H%C3%B6ffer" title="Paul Höffer">Paul Höffer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conducting</a> with <a href="/wiki/Clemens_Krauss" title="Clemens Krauss">Clemens Krauss</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a> with <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Arrau" title="Claudio Arrau">Claudio Arrau</a>.  Later, he took private classes with <a href="/wiki/Tor_Mann" title="Tor Mann">Tor Mann</a> in Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-SMIC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMIC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Teaching at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karl_Wohlfarts_Musikschule&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karl Wohlfarts Musikschule (page does not exist)">Karl Wohlfarts Musikschule</a> from 1939 to 1945, von Koch also spent the final two years of this period working as a sound expert and choirmaster for radio broadcasting.  He composed much music for the Swedish film industry.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> From 1953 to 1975, he was lecturer in <a href="/wiki/Harmony" title="Harmony">harmony</a> at the Stockholm Conservatory,<sup id="cite_ref-Swedishmusic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swedishmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> where he was appointed a professor in 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-SMIC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMIC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Von Koch became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Music">Royal Swedish Academy of Music</a> in 1957. He has received numerous other honors and prizes at both national and international levels for his compositions. He has written six <a href="/wiki/Symphony" title="Symphony">symphonies</a> (of which the fifth, <i>Lapponica</i>, is dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Sami_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Sami people">Sami people</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> twelve Scandinavian Dances, one <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> (<i>Pelle Svanslös</i>), and five <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballets</a>, as well as music for <a href="/wiki/Wind_instrument" title="Wind instrument">wind</a> <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestra</a>.
</p><p>Even in his nineties he composed/studied every day. His works can be described as uncomplicated and his motto was always to "keep the melody".<sup id="cite_ref-SMIC_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMIC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
