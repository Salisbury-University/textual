	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading mw-first-heading"><span class="mw-page-title-main">Nissanka Latha Mandapaya</span></h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="vector-body">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg/220px-Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg/330px-Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg/440px-Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2590" data-file-height="1824" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nissanka_Latha_Mandapaya.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nissanka Latha Mandapaya</div></div></div>
<p><b>Nissanka Latha Mandapaya</b> (<a href="/wiki/Sinhala_language" title="Sinhala language">Sinhala</a>: <span title="Sinhala-language text"><span lang="si">නිශ්ශංක ලතා මණ්ඩපය</span></span>) is a unique structure in the ancient city of <a href="/wiki/Polonnaruwa" title="Polonnaruwa">Polonnaruwa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>. 
A <i>màndapa</i> is a pillared structure that is open on all sides and protects the person(s) inside from the sun with a roof. By definition, as of the 20th century, <i>mándapas</i>, as temporary structures, are built inside a house or a building and serve as recitation platform during remembrance ceremonies for the dead.
</p><p>Built by King <a href="/wiki/Nissanka_Malla" class="mw-redirect" title="Nissanka Malla">Nissanka Malla</a> (1187-1196) and named after him, it is located near the western entrance of the <i>Dalada Maluva</i>, the area that contains the oldest and most sacred monuments in the city. A nearby stone inscription identifies this as the building used by Nissanka Malla to listen to <i>pirith</i> (chanting of Buddhist scriptures).<sup id="cite_ref-pk_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The structure is an elevated stone platform with a number of stone columns and surrounded by a low stone wall. These stone columns are the unique feature about the Nissanka Latha Manadapaya, since they are carved in a manner that is found nowhere else in the country. The eight <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> columns are arranged in two rows, with four in each row.<sup id="cite_ref-am_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-am-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Presumably used to support a roof,<sup id="cite_ref-historyofart_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyofart-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> each of them is approximately 8&#160;feet 4&#160;inches (2.54&#160;m) in height. In each of these columns, the crown is carved in the shape of a blossoming lotus bud. The rest of the column is elaborately carved to resemble the stem of the flower.<sup id="cite_ref-trends_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trends-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Unlike stone columns commonly seen in the architecture of this period, these are not straight, but are curved in three places. According to archaeologist <a href="/wiki/Senarath_Paranavithana" title="Senarath Paranavithana">Senarath Paranavithana</a>, the stone columns at the Nissanka Latha Mandapaya are the best examples of this feature of ancient Sri Lankan architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-am_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-am-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the center of the platform, flanked by the stone columns, appears a small stupa. This is also made from stone, but the top part of it has been destroyed. Its base is decorated with a carved design. The platform is surrounded by a stone railing. The structure is entered through a single stone doorway. In contrast to the elaborately carved stone pillars, these have an undecorated and plain finish.<sup id="cite_ref-am_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-am-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
